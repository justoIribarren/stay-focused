Manage your productivity time with the Pomodoro Technique

What is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique is a popular time management system that involves working/studying in 25-minute intervals with short 5-minute breaks in between to maximize focus. This technique was developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s while he was a university student. He used a tomato-shaped timer from his kitchen to time himself. After repeated trial and error, tweaking the amount of time, he eventually put together the Pomodoro Technique.

How to use the Pomodoro Timer?

  1. 1. List the tasks you want to accomplish today in order of priority.
  2. 2. Set the timer for 25 minutes.
  3. 3. Pick a task to work on.
  4. 4. Start the timer and concentrate on the task until the time ends
  5. 5. Take a break for 5 minutes
  6. 6. Take a longer break (15-30 minutes) after four pomodoros.
  7. 7. Repeat the cycle until you complete all the tasks.

Benefits of the Technique

  • - Improves focus: This technique lets you break the habit of multitasking. It trains you to be able to focus on one task at a time with reasonable time intervals.
  • - Facilitates project planning: After getting used to doing pomodoros, you’ll be able to plan out tasks and projects with more precision.
  • - Decreases mental fatigue: A study found that doing prolonged tasks leads to a decrease in performance, while short breaks between tasks can help you keep the focus.
  • - Sustains motivation: Breaking down tasks and tackling them in short bursts, you’ll feel a sense of satisfaction seeing how much you can accomplish in 25 minutes.
